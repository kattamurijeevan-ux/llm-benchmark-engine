Key findings
Qwen-32b reasoning anomaly
Qwen-32b scores 85% on reasoning but only 35% on math and 25% on medical. Its thinking-token architecture excels at factual recall but fails at structured numeric output — our #### parser doesn't match its format, inflating the math failure rate.
llama3-70b dominates structured tasks
Llama 3.3 70B is the clear winner on tasks requiring formatted output: 95% math, 70% medical, 70% coding. It also delivers the fastest latency on medical (170ms) despite being the largest model — Groq's LPU hardware advantage.
Size ≠ speed on math
llama3-8b takes 2369ms on math vs 669ms for llama3-70b — the smaller model generates longer chain-of-thought reasoning before reaching the answer, costing more time despite fewer parameters. Latency is not simply correlated with model size.
